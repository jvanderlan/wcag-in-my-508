1 Perceivable

1.1 Text Alternatives

1.1.1 Non-text Content: All non-text content should have a text equivalent.

1.2 Time-based Media

1.2.1 Audio-only and Video-only (Prerecorded): Audio only content should have a transcript. If there are multiple speakers the transcript should indicate who said what. If video, there should be a text equivalent of what happens in the video.

1.2.2 Captions (Prerecorded): Timed Captions should be provided for all important audio.

1.2.3 Audio Description or Media Alternative (Prerecorded): Provide descriptive alternative to what's happening in video, or a media alternative.

1.2.4 Captions (Live): Live broadcasts need to have timed text alternative of who is speaking, what they're saying and any important sounds.

1.2.5 Audio Description (Prerecorded): Similar to 1.2.3 but audio descript must be provided, not just Media Alternative

1.3 Adaptable

1.3.1 Info and Relationships: The structure of the DOM should allow meaningful relationships. e.g. input labels, table headers, aria roles, etc.

1.3.2 Meaningful Sequence: The structure of the DOM and use of CSS should not interfere with the meaning of the information. Don't use css floats to change order of information.

1.3.3 Sensory Characteristics: Instructions provided for understanding and operating content do not rely solely on sensory characteristics of components such as shape, size, visual location, orientation, or sound. "Click the round button"

1.4 Distinguishable

1.4.1 Use of Color: Color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information, indicating an action, prompting a response, or distinguishing a visual element.

1.4.2 Audio Control: If any audio on a Web page plays automatically for more than 3 seconds, either a mechanism is available to pause or stop the audio, or a mechanism is available to control audio volume independently from the overall system volume level.

1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum): The visual presentation of text and images of text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1

1.4.4 Resize text: Except for captions and images of text, text can be resized without assistive technology up to 200 percent without loss of content or functionality.

1.4.5 Images of Text: Try not to present text as an image. Logotypes are an exception.
